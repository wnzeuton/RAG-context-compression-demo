Title: Mei-Lin Zhao
Type: Biography

Introduction
Mei-Lin Zhao (born 2002) is a Taiwanese cognitive scientist, computer engineer, and academic researcher known for her foundational contributions to human-centered artificial intelligence, adaptive interface design, and AI ethics. Her work focuses on improving transparency, trust, and accessibility in automated systems, particularly in education and healthcare. Across research, policy advising, and mentorship, Zhao has played a central role in shaping global standards for responsible human–AI interaction.
---
Early Life and Education (2030)
Mei-Lin Zhao was born in 2002 in Taipei, Taiwan. She pursued interdisciplinary studies in cognitive science and computer engineering, developing an early interest in how humans perceive, interpret, and trust intelligent systems. Her academic work centered on human–AI interaction, adaptive cognition, and interface responsiveness. By 2030, Zhao had begun formal research on adaptive interfaces, exploring how systems could dynamically respond to user behavior while remaining interpretable and ethically grounded.
